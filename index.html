garden-ache

This project started in March 2020 where I spent my time with two 4’x8’ patches of land in central Indiana. For several days, I hacked at the ground with an energy that I don’t believe came from within myself. I think the willingness for plants to be domesticated into a garden is an incredibly generous entity that provides a sense of meaning and wholeness to human life. Then the ache I kept feeling to go back to the garden was one that was entirely unfamiliar. I documented my thoughts when I was in the garden and when I was away from it. I made a point to indulge in what was interesting to me. What exists now is a garden well under way for the growing season, and a series of photos, drawings, poems, and thoughts that try to grasp at the following questions: What draws me to the garden? What is my relationship witht the land? What kind of relationship do I want to have with the land? How do I reconcile that with my actions? How do we cultivate garden ethics?
