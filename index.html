garden-ache

This project started in March 2020 where I spent my time with two 4’x8’ patches of land in central Indiana. For several days, I hacked at the ground with an energy that I don’t believe came from within myself. I think the willingness for plants to be domesticated into a garden is an incredibly generous entity that provides a sense of meaning and wholeness to human life. Then the ache I kept feeling to go back to the garden was one that was entirely unfamiliar. I documented my thoughts when I was in the garden and when I was away from it. I made a point to indulge in what was interesting to me. What exists now is a garden well under way for the growing season, and a series of photos, drawings, poems, and thoughts that try to grasp at the following questions:
What draws me to the garden?
What is my relationship witht the land?
What kind of relationship do I want to have with the land?
How do I reconcile that with my actions?
How do we cultivate garden ethics?

gardenache


the way of gardenache is
how I once planted a dandelion thinking it would grow
for me.
of course it did not grow,
for dandelion does not respond to summoning.
remain silent and one may find you at your doorstep.
dig it out and the roots will burrow deeper.
two dandelions before next Tuesday

gardenache comes
with a ringing in the left ear.
remedy with the crumbling of soil clumps between the fingertips, then
indulge in the desire to build a garden.

when the soil becomes too fine to pinch,
study the motion of an earthworm to
remedy the elasticity of your doubt.

the first sprout will give you a moment of relief
that a sum of money and eleven meals of calories
have not been wasted,
and that the earth you poked and scraped and tore and hacked at
is intensely forgiving
as long as you listen.

so when the ringing creeps into to the right ear,
return to dandelion for advice.
